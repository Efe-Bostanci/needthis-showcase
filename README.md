# NeedThis.app

> **App idea marketplace** a platform where developers post app ideas they wish existed, and other developers can claim and build them.

🔗 **Shipped:** [needthis.app](https://needthis.app)

---

## Concept

The core insight: there are thousands of people who have clear, validated app ideas but can't build them and thousands of developers looking for project ideas worth their time. NeedThis connects them.

- Post an idea with a description and what you'd pay for it
- Browse ideas sorted by demand / votes
- Purchase the rights to build a specific idea (Lemon Squeezy checkout)

---

## Stack

**Frontend:** Next.js 14 · TypeScript · Tailwind CSS 
**Backend:** Supabase (Postgres + Auth + Storage) 
**Payments:** Lemon Squeezy 
**Deployment:** Vercel 

---

## What I Learned

NeedThis was built and launched in a short cycle as a solo project. After launch, organic traction was minimal the distribution strategy (Reddit cold posts) didn't convert. The project was sunset in favor of Councily.ai.

This is documented here intentionally: **shipping and learning from a zero-traction launch is part of the process.**

Key takeaways:
- Idea marketplaces need a two-sided cold-start strategy you can't launch to both sides simultaneously
- Reddit distribution requires community trust built before product launch
- Lemon Squeezy + Supabase is a fast, solid stack for solo SaaS
